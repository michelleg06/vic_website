## About the program

Summer of Science / Verano de las Ciencias pairs Indigenous postgraduate students with global mentors to co-develop research projects that combine diverse research methods, Indigenous knowledge systems, and locally defined priorities. Each cohort culminates in a poster presentation at the annual **EAAMO conference**.

## Background

The program launched in **2022** as a collaboration between **COPOCYT** (Consejo Potosino de Ciencia y Tecnología) and **MD4SG** (Mechanism Design for Social Good), with an initial focus on Indigenous women postgraduate students from **San Luis Potosí, Mexico**.

Over four iterations, the program has expanded to include:

- Science and technology councils from additional Mexican states
- **EAAMO-Bridges** as a core institutional partner
- An Amazonian Indigenous-driven sciences program based in **Peru** (added in the most recent two iterations)
- Other collaborating academic and community institutions

## Programme structure

- **Mentorship** — Each student is paired with a global mentor affiliated with a partner institution.
- **Projects** — Students design and carry out projects grounded in their communities, integrating Indigenous knowledge with academic research methods.
- **Output** — Projects culminate in poster presentations at the annual EAAMO conference.
